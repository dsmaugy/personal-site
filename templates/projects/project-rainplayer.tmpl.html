ambient music player/visualizer

movement

Rainplayer draws the dots on screen using a series of randomly generated BÃ©zier curves. The amplitude of the audio affects the speed of how fast the dot travels along its curve. Once a dot has reached the end of its path, a new curve is generated starting at its current position. This creates the "jumping" effect that occurs during the audio peaks.

rain sounds

The physical box controls the attributes of the ambient rainstorm in the background. The volume of the rain can be adjusted and thunderstorm claps can be summoned at will.
